About Kanyakumari

Kanyakumari, located at the southernmost tip of India, is where the Bay of Bengal, Arabian Sea, and Indian Ocean converge. Known for its breathtaking sunrises and sunsets, the town holds spiritual, cultural, and natural significance. It is a place where you can witness the unique spectacle of the sun and moon simultaneously visible on the horizon.

The town is named after Goddess Kanya Kumari, believed to be an incarnation of Parvati. Tourists flock here to see the famous Vivekananda Rock Memorial, Thiruvalluvar Statue, and the picturesque beaches that offer tranquility and inspiration.
